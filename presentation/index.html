The Models
Can we predict sleep from lifestyle?
Model Duration R² Consistency R²
Ridge / Lasso (Phase 1) 0.071 0.136

5-fold cross-validation · Adding HR, SES, and self-rated health drove the improvement
Interpreting the Results
"R²=0.18 doesn't mean our model failed.
It means sleep is genuinely complex."
Genetics, stress, medications, and environment are unmeasured.
A 16% improvement from adding 3 features is a meaningful scientific signal.
Consistent with Patel et al. 2012 St-Onge et al. 2016 Chinoy et al. 2021

Finding 4 · Feature Importance

The Biggest Finding
44% of sleep consistency is explained by
age × daily steps
Physical activity's effect on sleep regularity is strongly age-dependent.
Active older adults sleep most consistently. Inactive younger adults are most erratic.

Finding 6 · Model Fairness
The model fails underrepresented groups
A Critical Limitation
40% worse accuracy for
underrepresented minorities
UBR R² = 0.075  vs  White R² = 0.125

The model trained on a 70% White cohort does not generalize equally.
This must be disclosed, addressed in future work, and never hidden.
Honest Assessment
What this study cannot claim
  • 🚫Causal claims — this is cross-sectional, not experimental
  • 🚫Generalizability — Fitbit wearers are healthier and higher-SES than average
  • 🚫Equal performance — 40% gap for UBR participants is a structural failure
  • 🚫Smoking/alcohol effects — survey concept IDs returned unreliable data
  • 🚫Night-level dynamics — participant-level aggregation loses within-person variance

Takeaways
What we found
  • ✅Lifestyle predicts sleep — HistGBM achieves R²=0.099 (duration) and R²=0.184 (consistency)
  • ✅Consistency > duration — behavioral factors shape regularity more than length
  • ✅Age × steps is king — 44% of consistency variance from one interaction term
  • ✅Four phenotypes exist — Short but Regular is the most clinically urgent (55% chronic deprivation)
  • ⚠️Fairness gap persists — 40% accuracy drop for UBR participants requires urgent attention
